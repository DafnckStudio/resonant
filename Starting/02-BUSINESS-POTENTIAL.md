### 1.2 The Problem

**Pour les Creators:**
- Outils AI fragmentés (un pour scripts, un pour SEO, un pour thumbnails)
- Pas de workflows réutilisables
- Output inconsistant entre sessions
- Burnout créatif

**Pour les Agences:**
- Impossible de scaler la production content
- Collaboration chaotique entre équipes
- Pas de version control sur les workflows
- Reporting manuel et time-consuming

**Pour les Enterprises:**
- Compliance impossible (pas d'audit trail)
- Brand consistency non garantie
- Lock-in sur un seul provider AI
- Pas d'intégration avec outils existants

## 7. Risks & Mitigations

### 7.1 Risk Matrix

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| **AI provider changes pricing** | High | High | Multi-provider, open-source fallback |
| **Google/Meta enters market** | Medium | High | Niche focus, speed, community |
| **Poppy copies features** | High | Medium | Execution speed, brand, moat |
| **Slow user adoption** | Medium | High | Strong PLG, freemium, virality |
| **Technical complexity** | Medium | High | Experienced team, proven stack |
| **Talent acquisition** | Medium | Medium | Remote-first, equity, mission |

### 7.2 Key Risks Deep Dive

**Risk 1: AI Provider Dependency**
- Mitigation: Architecture multi-provider dès le jour 1
- Fallback: Open-source models (Llama, Mixtral)
- Hedge: Coûts AI indexés dans pricing

**Risk 2: Market Competition**
- Mitigation: First-mover sur visual workflows + multi-AI
- Differentiation: Focus creator economy vs enterprise-only
- Speed: Ship weekly, iterate fast

**Risk 3: Technical Execution**
- Mitigation: Proven stack (Next.js, Convex, React Flow)
- Validation: Architecture déjà prototypée
- Team: Hire senior engineers only
